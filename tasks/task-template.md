# Title

One-line statement of intent: what this task is fundamentally about and why it
matters — a sentence that adds information beyond the title. Surfaced as the
task's workboard label, so make it count.

## Current Reality
What exists now. The present state, relevant artifacts, constraints, observed behavior, and context.

## Desired Reality
What should be true when the task is complete.

## Gap Analysis
The difference between current and desired reality. What must change, be discovered, or decided.

## Known Facts / Assumptions / Unknowns
Separate grounded facts from working assumptions and unresolved questions.

## Investigations
Bounded research or inspection work needed to reduce uncertainty.

## Models / Forecasts / Risks
Predictions, design models, likely failure modes, and premortems.

## Transformations
Concrete changes to code, docs, process, architecture, or artifacts.

## Evidence
How completion will be verified. Tests, commands, observations, review criteria, or acceptance checks.

## Decisions
Decisions made during the task, with enough rationale to preserve intent.

## Open Fronts
Still-active threads that are not yet resolved.

## Next Actions
Executable leaves: the next concrete actions an agent or human can take.
